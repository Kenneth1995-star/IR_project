Kubera Kuchela (1943 film)

Kuchela(Papanasam Sivan), Lord Krishna's(P. S. Govindan) boyhood friend acquires wealth, which goes to his dead. Driven by grad and ambition. Kuchela transforms himself into Kubera(P. U. Chinnappa). In the film, there are three Kuberas - Kubera the lord of wealth(D. Balasubramaniam), Kuchela who transformed himself into Kubera(P. U. Chinnappa) and the third one, a demon transformed into Kubera(P. U. Chinnappa again). Mean while, Lord Krishna, creates a lovely women(T. R. Rajakumari) out of a flower. Complications ensue with more than one Kubera falling love for her. The "Original" Kubera steps into establish his supremacy and more complications arise. However, the lord Krishna has his own bag of tricks and settles the confusion bringing the lovers (P. U. Chinnappa and T. R. Rajakumari) together.[1] The comic relief was provided as usual by N. S. Krishnan, T. A. Mathuram and T. S. Durairaj. In one comic song N. S. Krishnan plays a female role. While Mathuram plays Krishnan.
