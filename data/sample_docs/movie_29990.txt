Manikuyil

Muthuvelu (Murali) lives with his mother Deivanai (Kokila) and his grandfather (A. K. Veerasamy) in a mountainous area. Despite being an educated man, he makes his living harvesting honey using traditional methods. The wicked forest officer Sundaram (Charan Raj) often gets into fight with Muthuvelu. One day, the city girl Kaveri (Saradha Preetha) spots Muthuvelu singing a song and she records it with her video camera, she then sends the video to a TV channel. Muthuvelu's song is telecast by the TV channel, Muthuvelu becomes popular in no time thus he becomes a singer. Once again, Muthuvelu and the forest officer Sundaram get into a fight, this time the culprit Sundaram is suspended from his job by his superiors.
Thereafter, Muthuvelu and Kaveri fall in love with each other. At first, Kaveri's father Ramasamy (Shanmugasundaram) accepts for their marriage. Sundaram enters their home pretending to be Muthuvelu's father. Deivanai claims that he is not her husband, when Kaveri's father Ramasamy asks about her husband's identity, she refuses to tell. Ramasamy puts his foot down when she refuses to reveal any informations about Muthuvelu's father. Ramasamy is now grateful to Sundaram and he appoints him to work in his factory.
Later that night, Deivanai reveals to her son Muthuvelu that she was an orphan and lived alone in the past. She had a secret affair with the wealthy man Ratnasabapathy (Vijayakumar), he promised her to marry her. Thereafter, Deivanai received death threats from Ratnasabapathy's relatives and she had no other choice than leaving the village. A kind-hearted person, now Muthuvelu's grandfather, helped Deivanai and he took care of her like his own daughter.
Muthuvelu goes in search of his father. He finds that his father Ratnasabapathy is now married and has three sons. What transpires later forms the crux of the story.
